Lesson 3: Understanding Debit and Credit

Introduction on Debit and Credit

In accounting, a debit is an entry that increases asset or expense accounts and decreases liability, revenue, and equity accounts, while a credit is an entry that decreases asset or expense accounts and increases liability, revenue, and equity accounts.
Together, debits and credits are used to ensure that the accounting equation remains balanced and accurately reflects financial transactions.

Equity Decrease Increase

What is Debit?

A debit increases the value of asset and expense accounts or decreases the value of liability, revenue, and equity accounts.

  • More on Debit
    • Assets: Debits increase the value of asset accounts.
    • Expenses: Debits increase expense accounts.
    • Liabilities: Debits decrease the value of liability accounts.
    • Revenue: Debits decrease revenue accounts.
    • Equity: Debits decrease equity accounts.

What is Credit?

A credit increases the value of liability, revenue, and equity accounts or decreases the value of asset and expense accounts.

  • More on Credit
    • Assets: Credits decrease the value of asset accounts.
    • Expenses: Credits decrease expense accounts.
    • Liabilities: Credits increase the value of liability accounts.
    • Revenue: Credits increase revenue accounts.
    • Equity: Credits increase equity accounts.
